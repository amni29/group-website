Food by Country

Food is one of the best ways to explore a country's culture! Each nation has its own unique culinary offerings.

KOREA

Bibimbap

Bibimbap (비빔밥) is probably one of the most well-known and beloved Korean dishes to many people. The term "bibim" means "mixing" and "bap" refers to cooked rice. Bibimbap is served as a bowl of warm white rice topped with seasoned vegetables, kimchi, gochujang and soy sauce. A raw or fried egg and sliced meat are common additions.

VIETNAM

Beef Pho Noodle

Pho most commonly refers to pho bo, or beef pho. Pho is a Vietnamese soup dish consisting of broth, rice noodles, herbs and meat.

THAILAND

Pad Kra Pao

Pad kra pow is one of the most popular dishes in Thai cuisine. It’s a Thai food favorite made with some type of seasoned ground meat – like chicken, or beef – stir-fried with Thai basil and garlic. In Thailand, pad kra pow is a popular lunch dish, something you can easily find at restaurants and food courts. It’s usually served with steamed white rice and topped with a Thai-style fried egg (with crispy browned edges).

JAPAN

Soba

Soba is a buckwheat noodle specialty of Japan. It’s extremely popular, and it’s served both in general noodle restaurants and in specialized ones. It’s also relatively simple to prepare at home, by getting the noodles and the soup in which they are dipped at a grocery store. Soba can be enjoyed in a cold dip, or a broth, as a noodle soup. This dish appears in different varieties depending on the season and the region you’re visiting.

INDIA

Chicken Masala

Chicken masala is a dish with flavoursome chicken coated with a delicious, spicy and thick masala. It can be served with steamed rice, flavoured rice, chapati and naan.

INDONESIA

Nasi Padang

Nasi padang, sometimes referred to as Padang rice, is a Minangkabau dish of steamed rice served with various choices of pre-cooked dishes originating from West Sumatra, Indonesia. It is named after the city of Padang, capital of the West Sumatra province. A miniature banquet of meats, fish, vegetables, and spicy sambals eaten with plain white rice, it is Sumatra's most famous export and the Minangkabau people's primary contribution to Indonesian cuisine. Nasi padang is found in various cities in Sumatra, Java, Kalimantan, Sulawesi, Nusa Tenggara, and Papua as well as neighboring countries Malaysia, Singapore and Australia.
